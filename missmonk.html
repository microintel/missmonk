The gap

Rarely how to live well.

The problem

Nobody assigns homework on trust

School covers science, maths, biology, chemistry, computer science in depth. But financial literacy, psychology, emotional intelligence, self-awareness and relationships usually show up only in adulthood — if at all.

So children absorb their beliefs about money and people from wherever is loudest: social media, hearsay, and misinformation. Left uncorrected, that gap shows up later as poor decisions, unhealthy relationships, and sometimes worse.

The response

Start the lesson earlier

Microintel built MissMonk to close that gap while it's still cheap to close — in childhood, not after the damage is done.

It compresses the best books on money, mind, and relationships into short summaries and reel-style lessons — built to be scrolled, not assigned. Not a book-summary app. A step toward a wiser generation.

English

MissMonk

School teaches science, maths, biology, chemistry, computer science — and rightly so. But life skills like financial literacy, psychology, emotional intelligence, self-awareness, relationships, trust and love are usually left for higher education, or never taught at all.

Without them, children fill the gap with whatever social media and misinformation offer — and that can lead to poor decisions, unhealthy relationships, and in some cases, crime, violence or abuse.

Microintel built MissMonk to turn the world's best books into simple summaries, interactive lessons, and short scrollable content — so learning about money, emotions, relationships and personal growth feels as easy as scrolling a reel. Not a book-summary app. A step toward a wiser generation.
