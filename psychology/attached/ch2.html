Chapter Two, Made Simple

Needing someone
is not weak

We are told to "stand on our own." But when life gets hard, one hand to hold can calm your whole body. This is not a flaw. This is how humans are built.

The old idea

"Don't depend on anyone"

For a long time, people were taught: be strong alone. Do not lean on your partner too much. Look after your own feelings, and let them look after theirs.

This sounds mature. But it is not true. When we love someone, our brain and body link with theirs. We are not built to be two separate islands. We are built to depend on each other — and that is not a problem to fix.

A real-life example

Fiona and Pranav on the race show

Fiona and Pranav were on a TV show where couples race around the world. Under stress, Fiona often needed Pranav to hold her hand. Pranav pulled back — it felt like giving in to her too much.

Later, Fiona blamed herself. "I was too needy," she said. "I should have stayed calm on my own." But that is not what really happened. Pranav also froze before a scary jump, and only calmed down once Fiona stood close and encouraged him. They both needed each other. Neither one was weak for it.

The big idea

The more you can safely depend on your partner,
the more brave and independent you become.

This is called the dependency paradox

This sounds strange at first. How can leaning on someone make you stronger on your own? But think about it simply: when you know someone has your back, you take more risks. You try the new job. You speak up. You explore. Without that person, you hold back and play it safe.

What science found

Your body feels fear go down

Scientists told women they might get a small electric shock, and scanned their brains at the same time. Fear showed up clearly on the scan — until someone held their hand.

This proves something simple: love is not only a feeling in your head. Holding hands with someone you trust actually calms your body. That comfort is real, not silly.

A simple picture

The child in the toy room

A baby sits in a room full of toys. While her mother is nearby, she crawls everywhere and plays freely. The moment her mother steps out, she stops playing and cries at the door. When her mother comes back, the baby calms down fast — and soon goes back to playing.

This safe feeling has a name: a secure base. It means having one person you can 100% count on. Adults need this too. When we know someone truly backs us, we feel free to explore our own life — new jobs, new goals, new dreams.

A person who feels backed by someone does not shrink from life. They step into it.
What happens without it

When a partner isn't there for you

Researchers also studied blood pressure in marriages. If your marriage feels good, simply being near your partner can bring your blood pressure down. But if the marriage feels unhappy, being near your partner can raise it instead — and it stays high as long as you're together.

↓

↑
Unhappy bond: partner stresses your body

So a good partner is not a "nice to have." A secure, dependable partner is linked to real health — a calmer mind and a calmer body.

What you can do

Simple rules to remember

Depending on someone you love is not a weakness. It is how humans are made.

Do

  • ✓Ask for a hand to hold, a hug, or a call when you're stressed. It really does calm your body.
  • ✓Let your partner know early when you need comfort, instead of waiting until you're upset.
  • ✓Be someone's secure base too — show up for them so they feel free to take risks.
  • ✓See a good, dependable partner as good for your health, not just your heart.

Don't

  • ✕Don't call yourself "too needy" for wanting support. That need is normal, not a flaw.
  • ✕Don't wait until you're falling apart to ask for comfort — ask early.
  • ✕Don't believe you must be 100% self-sufficient to be "healthy." That idea isn't biology.
  • ✕Don't ignore it if being close to your partner keeps stressing you, not calming you.
