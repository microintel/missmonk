Chapter One

There are 3 ways to love

From a young age, your brain learns a pattern. This pattern decides how you act when you get close to someone. It decides how safe closeness feels to you. It decides how you handle a fight. It decides how you feel when your partner goes quiet.

Scientists found 3 main styles. Almost everyone fits into one of them.

Vignesh — Avoidant

Vignesh likes his freedom. When someone gets too close, he feels stuck. This does not mean he loves less. Closeness just feels unsafe to him.

A common story

Elsa and Vignesh

Elsa liked Vignesh from the start. He was warm and sweet at first. But as she got closer, he pulled back. He got "busy." His texts got shorter. The more distant he became, the more Elsa checked her phone and worried what she did wrong.

Here is the twist. Vignesh did not pull away because Elsa was not good enough. He pulled away because things got too close, and closeness scares him. Elsa was not "too much" either. Her worry is just how an anxious heart reacts to distance. Neither one did anything wrong. They just speak two different love languages.

Two good people, stuck in a pattern that was never really about either of them.

Why we are like this

This is very old wiring

Long ago, staying close to someone who cared for you kept you alive. So our brains learned to hold on tight to the people we love. That old habit is still inside us today. It just shows up in dating and marriage now, instead of survival in the wild.

Your style can also change. It is shaped by how you were raised, but also by your life and the people you meet. About 1 in 4 people change their style over a few years, often without even noticing.

What you can do

Simple rules to remember

You cannot change your style overnight. But you can stop it from controlling you.

Do

  • ✓Learn your own style first. You cannot fix what you cannot see.
  • ✓Say what you need in simple words, like Lisa does. Do not wait for your partner to guess.
  • ✓If someone pulls away every time you get close, notice that. It tells you something real.
  • ✓It is okay to need love and closeness. That is not weakness.
  • ✓Look for a partner whose actions match their words.

Don't

  • ✕Don't confuse worry with love. "I can't stop thinking about them" can just mean fear, not real love.
  • ✕Don't keep chasing someone who keeps pulling away, hoping they will change.
  • ✕Don't blame yourself when a partner needs space. It is often not about you.
  • ✕Don't think love alone can fix two very different attachment styles.
  • ✕Don't ignore a pattern that keeps repeating in your relationships. It is not bad luck.
