The Story

Two friends, one salary

Vignesh and Pranav joined the same company on the same day, on the same salary. Ten years on, people assume Vignesh is the richer one. They're wrong.

₹0 savings · ₹18L car loan

Pranav

Still drives his first Swift. No one's impressed at the society meetings. He quietly runs SIPs every month and never talks about it.

Vignesh isn't rich — he just spent his salary faster than he earned it. Pranav isn't boring — he's the one actually building wealth. You can only see one of them clearly, and it's the wrong one.

Take This With You

4 lessons from this chapter

1What you see is never the full picture. A ₹1 crore car tells you the owner has ₹1 crore less, not more.
2Being rich is easy to spot. Being wealthy is invisible, on purpose — it's money that was never converted into "stuff."
3Wealth = income − ego. The gap between what you could spend and what you choose to spend is where wealth is built.
4Stop learning from people who look successful. You can't copy what you can't see — so build your own quiet discipline instead.
