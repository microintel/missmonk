Positive Psychology · Simple Guide

You deserve the best.
Here's how to feel it.

Six small, real ideas about noticing good things, being kind to yourself, and caring for others — each with a quick example first, then the point. Scroll down and watch the jar fill up.

01 · Before you sleep
Every night, Vignesh thinks of three good things from his day — even something small like "I had a nice lunch." Then he writes them down.

Find three good things every day, and write them down.

They don't need to be big. Small good moments count too.

02 · Ask "why"
Pranav got a compliment at work. Instead of just moving on, he asks himself: "Why did that happen?" He realises he planned his week smartly.

Ask yourself why the good thing happened.

This trains your brain to notice good cause and effect — not just good luck.

03 · Karaoke night
Riya sings badly at karaoke and feels embarrassed. Instead of only thinking about that, she reminds herself: "I can't sing, but I'm a great cook and a good friend."

Remind yourself of your real good qualities, not just your flaws.

This is called self-affirmation — and it only works if the good thing is actually true.

04 · A hard test
Pranav does badly on a math test and feels discouraged. Before the next test, he spends a moment thinking about something he truly values — and does much better this time.

Thinking about what you value helps you bounce back from failure.

A 2009 study found this simple habit made people perform better after a setback.

05 · Helping a friend
Riya spends her Saturday helping a friend move house instead of shopping for herself. She ends the day feeling happier than any purchase could make her feel.

Being kind to others makes you happier than money does.

Gratitude, optimism, and kindness are three of the biggest predictors of happiness.

06 · A bad moment
Vignesh misses his bus and thinks, "I'm so stupid." Then he pauses, remembers a value he's proud of, sits with that thought for a moment — and feels calmer, even though the bus is still gone.

When an upsetting thought hits, pause and recall something good about yourself.

The upset doesn't disappear — but it stops running the whole show.

Main Lesson

Fixing what's wrong is not enough — you also need to notice what's good, and share it.

A garden that only gets weeded, never gets sunlight, still won't grow. Your mind is the same — pulling out problems isn't enough. It also needs light: good things noticed, kindness given, and good qualities remembered.
