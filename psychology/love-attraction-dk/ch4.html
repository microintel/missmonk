Your Mental Map
of Love

Before you ever fell in love, you already drew a map for it — without knowing you were doing it.

Since you were little, you've been quietly answering two questions: "Am I worth loving?" and "Can other people be trusted?" Your answers became a map. And without noticing, you use that same old map every time you fall for someone.

"Early attachment relations come to form a prototype for later relationships outside the family." Kim Bartholomew & Leonard M. Horowitz
1
Secure

Vignesh's parents always listened when he was upset as a kid. Now, in relationships, he trusts easily and doesn't panic when his partner needs space.

When your needs are met growing up, you learn you're worth loving and people can be trusted.

2
Anxious

Pranav's home was loving one day, distant the next. Now he re-reads texts ten times, scared his partner is about to leave.

When love felt unpredictable, you may believe others are fine — but something is wrong with you. That's anxiety in love.

3
Avoidant

Riya's parents were rarely there emotionally. She decided, "I'll handle myself." Now closeness makes her want to pull back.

When people kept letting you down, you learn to trust yourself but not others — so intimacy starts to feel risky.

4
Fearful

Someone hurt badly by the very people meant to protect them may want closeness — and be terrified of it — at the same time.

When trust in yourself and others both get hurt, you can want love and fear it in the same breath.

5
Healing

Vignesh's friend had a rough childhood, but is happily married today — he went to therapy and worked through the old wounds first.

A hard start doesn't fix your future. The map can be redrawn — therapy and CBT genuinely help.

6
Self-Compassion

Riya started telling herself "I deserve good things" every time she doubted it — instead of waiting for someone else to prove it.

Knowing your pattern is step one. Being kind to yourself is what actually starts to change it.

Main Lesson

You didn't choose your first map of love —
but you can redraw it.

Think of your childhood map like an old GPS. It got you around fine when you were a kid — that's all it knew. But some roads on it are outdated now. The moment you notice it's leading you the wrong way in love, you can update it.
