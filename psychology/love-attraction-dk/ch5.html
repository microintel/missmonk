Cognitive Behavioral Therapy, simplified

Your mind isn't broken.
It's just tangled.

Sometimes one small thought spirals into a bad day. That's not weakness — it's a pattern. And patterns can be traced, questioned, and gently rewired.

↓ keep going, it gets clearer
The idea

A negative thought shows up. It makes you feel something painful. That feeling changes how you act. And how you act quietly confirms the original thought — so it comes back stronger. CBT calls this the negativity trap, and the whole trick is catching it at the very first step: the thought.

How it spirals

One thought pulls the rest along

Thoughts, feelings, actions, and physical sensations aren't separate — they're wired together in a loop. Break it anywhere, and the whole cycle loosens.

01 — Thought

The spark

A single sentence your mind decides to believe.

"I'm so unattractive."

02 — Feeling

The weight

The thought lands in your body as an emotion.

Inadequate. Sad. Ashamed.

03 — Sensation

The static

Emotion shows up physically before you even notice.

Listless. Vulnerable. Heavy.

04 — Behavior

The retreat

You act in a way that matches the story — and proves it right.

Staying in. Avoiding people. Hiding.

And the loop feeds itself — unless you interrupt it at the thought.
Ten familiar traps

Ways your brain bends the truth

These aren't character flaws — they're mental shortcuts everyone's brain takes under stress. Spotting the pattern is most of the work.

⚫⚪

All-or-nothing thinking

You see life in only two settings: perfect, or complete failure. No in-between exists.

"I missed one workout, so my whole fitness plan is ruined."

🔁

Overgeneralizing

One bad moment gets rebranded as "this always happens to me."

"I failed one interview — I'll never get a job."

🔍

Mental filter

You zoom in on the single bad detail and let it blur out everything good.

You score 95 out of 100, and can only think about the 5 marks you lost.

🙈

Disqualifying the positive

Good things happen, but your mind finds a reason they don't really count.

"They only complimented me because they were being polite."

🔮

Jumping to conclusions

You guess what people think, or how things will turn out, with zero real evidence.

"My friend hasn't replied yet — they must be angry with me."

🔬

Magnifying & minimizing

Problems balloon into disasters, while your wins shrink down to nothing.

"One typo in my presentation ruined everything" — even though it went well.

💭

Emotional reasoning

If it feels true, your mind treats it as fact — no questions asked.

"I feel anxious, so something bad must be about to happen."

📏

"Should" statements

You hold yourself — and everyone else — to rules that were never realistic.

"I should never make mistakes."

🏷️

Labeling

One moment gets stretched into your entire identity.

"I forgot my keys again — I'm just so careless."

🎯

Personalization

You quietly take the blame for things that were never really yours to carry.

"My teammate seems quiet today — I must have offended them."

The way out

A five-question reset

Next time a thought pulls you under, try walking through these — slowly, honestly, in your own head or on paper.

01

Name it. What's the exact thought or belief that's bothering you right now?

02

Rate it. On a scale of 0–100%, how much do you actually believe it's true?

03

Spot the trap. Does it match one of the ten patterns above?

04

Reframe it. Is there a kinder, more balanced way to read the same situation?

05

Rate it again. Even a small drop in belief is real progress.

You don't need to force the number down to zero. A small dent, repeated often enough, is exactly how a mind quietly rewires itself into a calmer one.

You're not thinking wrong.
You're just thinking human.

Every mind takes shortcuts under pressure. Noticing the shortcut is the whole first step back to solid ground.
