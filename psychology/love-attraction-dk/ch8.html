On love, freedom & everything between

Does love mean giving up your independence?

Most of us secretly fear that answer is yes. It isn't. Here's what really happens when two people stay close — without either one disappearing.

keep going
The big idea

Real love is not about losing yourself. It's about feeling safe enough to be yourself.

Here are 7 simple ideas that explain this. Each one has an everyday example — read the example first, the idea will make sense right away.

1

Love does not mean losing your independence

Myth: "If I fall in love, I'll lose my freedom."
Truth: Healthy love lets you keep your own life AND have a partner you can rely on.

ExampleRiya goes on a solo trip with her friends. Her partner doesn't stop her — he cheers her on. She has her own life. She's still close to him. Both, at once.

2

Safe haven

A safe haven is the person you run to when life feels hard.

ExampleYou fail an exam. You feel low. You call your best friend or your partner. Just talking to them makes you feel calmer.

3

Secure base

A secure base is someone whose support gives you the confidence to face challenges.

ExampleBefore a job interview, Vignesh's partner tells him, "You've prepared well. You'll do great." He walks in feeling more confident — because someone believes in him.

4

Anxious attachment

Some people often worry about being rejected or left behind.

ExamplePranav's partner doesn't reply for three hours. His mind jumps straight to, "Maybe he's angry with me."

5

Avoidant attachment

Some people find it hard to depend on others, even when they need help.

ExampleVignesh is stressed all week. A friend asks, "Are you okay?" He says, "I'm fine" — even though he really isn't.

6

Self-soothing

It's healthy to calm yourself down, instead of needing someone else for every feeling.

ExampleInstead of sending ten worried texts, Riya takes a walk, breathes slowly, and listens to music — then talks to her partner once she's calm.

7

Small reassurance matters

Sometimes one small message is enough to make someone feel safe again.

Thinking of you.
Hope your meeting goes well.
I'll call you tonight.
Miss you.
The main lesson

Healthy love is interdependence

  • You support each other.
  • You trust each other.
  • You keep your own identity.
  • You can ask for help without becoming completely dependent.

Two trees, growing side by side

Their roots support each other underground. But each tree still stands on its own. That's what healthy love looks like.

Interdependence, not dependence.
