A simple guide to human values

The two voices inside you

Every day, two things speak inside you: what you actually do — and what you truly wish you'd do. When they agree, you feel light. When they fight, you feel heavy. This little guide shows you how to listen to both, in plain words.

Part One

What is "looking inside yourself"?

Looking inside yourself simply means checking things out for yourself, instead of just believing what someone else told you. Nobody can hand you the truth about your own life. You have to see it for yourself — the same way you'd taste food to know if it's good, instead of trusting someone else's opinion of it.

Think of it like this: someone can point at the moon, but you still have to look up yourself to actually see it. This whole page is just a finger pointing. The seeing part is yours to do.

Part Three

Your built-in compass

You already have a compass inside you that quietly knows what's right for you — long before anyone teaches you. We call it your natural acceptance. It doesn't change with your mood, your city, or your age. Tap each question and answer it honestly in your head before reading the note.

Part Four

How to check any idea for yourself

Whenever someone tells you something — a rule, an idea, even something in this page — don't just believe it or reject it. Test it, step by step. Tap each step to open it.

Treat it as a suggestion, not a fact ⌄

Don't swallow it whole, and don't throw it out either. Just hold it loosely, like a suggestion someone made to you, waiting to be checked.

Check it against your compass ⌄

Ask: does this feel right deep down, or not? You already know the answer, if you're honest with yourself.

Try living it out ⌄

Actually behave this way for a while — with people, and with the world around you (your things, your surroundings, nature).

Watch what it leads to ⌄

Does it make you and the other person happier? Does it make things around you better, not worse? If yes on both counts, it's a good idea to keep.

Pranav's friend forgot his birthday. Part of him wanted to stay angry for days. But deep down, he knew that carrying anger only made him feel heavy — not his friend. He spoke about it calmly instead, and felt light again.

In Short

What to do, and what to avoid

✓ Do this

  • Pause and check how something really makes you feel, before deciding.
  • Treat every rule or opinion — even this page — as something to verify, not swallow.
  • Notice when you feel uneasy inside — it's usually your compass, not just a mood.
  • Aim for outcomes where both sides feel good, not just you.
  • Be patient with yourself — this is a slow, lifelong habit, not a one-day fix.

✕ Avoid this

  • Don't accept an idea just because it's popular, old, or said with confidence.
  • Don't confuse a personal liking (like a favourite food) with your real compass.
  • Don't ignore that uneasy feeling and push through anyway — it usually comes back later.
  • Don't chase what's easy or profitable if it quietly feels wrong.
  • Don't expect this to work by only thinking about it — you have to actually live it out.
