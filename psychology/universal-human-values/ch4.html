A simple guide to human values

Happiness is not something
you chase. It is a state you're in.

When what you feel inside matches what you truly want — that's called harmony. And harmony is what we call happiness. This page explains that idea in plain words, with real-life feeling, not theory.

↓ scroll slowly, and think as you read
01 — The Idea

Happiness = Harmony.
Unhappiness = Disharmony.

Inside you, there are two things: what you are (your thoughts, wants, choices) and what naturally feels right to you (your natural acceptance — like respect, trust, love). When these two match, you feel light and calm. When they clash, you feel restless, even if nothing "bad" is happening outside.

02 — In Real Life

Three people, three feelings

Here's how this idea shows up in ordinary days. See if you recognise yourself in one of them.

P

Pranav's exam result

Pranav topped his class and everyone praised him. For a day he felt on top of the world. But by the next week, the excitement had faded and he felt empty again — because praise from others is borrowed happiness. It runs out. It was never really his.

V

Vignesh and the new phone

Vignesh saved up and bought the phone he always wanted. It felt amazing on day one. By day thirty, it was just a phone again — nothing new, nothing exciting. The object didn't change. His feeling did. That's the difference between excitement and real happiness.

M

Mahadevaswamy's quiet evening

Mahadevaswamy didn't get a promotion or a gift that day. He simply sat with his family, feeling genuine care for them, and being cared for back. No big event happened — yet he felt deeply content. That's harmony in relationship, and it doesn't fade the way excitement does.

"You cannot give what you don't have. If you don't have peace within, you cannot give peace to another."

03 — A Common Mix-up

Excitement is not happiness

We often mix these two up. Excitement comes from outside — a taste, a compliment, a purchase. It always fades, because your body and mind get tired of the same sensation. Try the slider below and watch what happens as you "eat" more of your favourite sweet.

The sweet didn't change. Your body's need for it did. This is true for anything outside you — food, phones, likes, praise. It can give a short spark, called excitement, but it can never give you continuous happiness. Only harmony within can do that — because it doesn't depend on running out.

04 — Where Harmony Lives

You live at four levels — bring harmony to all of them

You are not just one person floating alone. You exist as an individual, inside a family, inside a society, and inside nature. Real, lasting happiness needs harmony at every one of these levels — not just one.

01

Myself

Peace between what I think, what I want, and what I actually do.

02

My family

Real care and trust with the people closest to me — not just living under one roof.

03

Society

Fair, respectful dealings with people around me — neighbours, friends, strangers.

04

Nature

Taking only what I need, and not damaging the air, water, and earth I depend on.

05 — The Other Half

Being rich is not the same as being prosperous

Prosperity is simply the feeling that you have enough — or more than enough — of what your body truly needs. It has nothing to do with the size of your bank balance.

A person with little, who knows their needs clearly and can meet them, feels prosperous and happily shares with others. A person with a great deal, who is never sure "how much is enough," feels deprived — and starts guarding, hoarding, or even taking from others out of that fear. Vignesh with his one working phone can feel more prosperous than someone with ten phones and constant worry about the next upgrade.

"A prosperous person thinks of sharing. A person who feels deprived thinks of grabbing more."

On the real meaning of wealth
06 — Putting It to Use

What to do, and what to avoid

You don't need to remember theory. Just remember this, and try it in daily life:

✓ Do this

  • Pause and check: is what I want in harmony with what feels right?
  • Value real relationships over praise and attention.
  • Ask "how much do I actually need?" before chasing more.
  • Enjoy food, gadgets, and comforts — but don't expect them to make you happy forever.
  • When you feel low, look for the disharmony causing it, instead of just distracting yourself.

✕ Avoid this

  • Don't confuse a good mood from a compliment with real happiness — it won't last.
  • Don't try to escape sadness with overeating, endless scrolling, or other habits — they only hide the feeling, not solve it.
  • Don't measure your worth or prosperity by how much you own.
  • Don't ignore harmony in your family or surroundings while chasing personal success alone.
  • Don't believe happiness must depend on someone else's approval — it's yours to build.
