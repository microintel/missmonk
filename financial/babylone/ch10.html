Part 1

The Story, Made Simple

An old, rich merchant named Sharru Nada is riding into Babylon with a spoiled young man, Hadan Gula, who thinks work is only for slaves. So Sharru Nada tells him the truth about his own past.

🐫 A Rich Man, A Lazy Boy

Sharru Nada is now a famous, wealthy trader. Hadan Gula, riding beside him, wears jewels and says work is "for slaves." Sharru Nada stays quiet — because he was once a slave himself, and work is the reason he isn't one anymore.

Setting: the road into Babylon

⛓️ Chained on the Road

Years ago, young Sharru Nada was sold into slavery because of his brother's crime. He marched to Babylon in chains next to three very different men — Megiddo, Zabado, and a man called Pirate.

Setting: the slave column, on the way to Babylon

🍞 A Lucky Break

At the slave market, most men were bought to build the city walls — brutal, deadly work. But Sharru Nada convinced a baker to buy him instead by promising to work hard. He believed this was his lucky break.

Setting: the slave market

🍯 Selling Honey Cakes

He asked his master for a side job: baking extra honey cakes and selling them on the street in his free time. His master agreed to share the profit. Slowly, coin by coin, his purse grew heavier.

Setting: the streets of Babylon

🕯️ Freedom, Lost and Found

Just when he was close to buying his freedom, his master's gambling debts caused him to be sold again — this time to dig canals in the brutal desert heat. He nearly broke. But his old friend Arad Gula (the rug seller who always bought his cakes) never stopped looking for him — and finally bought his freedom and made him a business partner.

Setting: the Grand Canal, then back home

Part 4 — Same Story, Today

This Still Happens Now

You don't need chains or deserts to see this pattern. It shows up in ordinary jobs and daily habits.

Vignesh — The Megiddo

Vignesh takes on the boring tasks at work without complaining and always finishes what he starts. A year later, he's the one his manager trusts with bigger projects.

Pranav — The Zabado

Pranav does the least he can get away with, hoping no one notices. He stays stuck in the same role while people around him grow.

Dhanush — The Pirate

Dhanush gets angry at every hard task and picks fights with his team instead of solving problems. He burns bridges he later needs.

Part 5 — Take This With You

What To Do, What Not To Do

Do this

  • Treat hard work as a friend, not a punishment
  • Do your best even when no one is watching
  • Use small side efforts to build something extra, like the honey cakes
  • Stay loyal to people who help you — it comes back around
  • Keep going even when results are slow to show

Don't do this

  • Don't do the least you can get away with
  • Don't let anger replace effort when things get hard
  • Don't wait for luck instead of building your own chances
  • Don't judge people only by where they started
  • Don't forget the people who believed in you early on
