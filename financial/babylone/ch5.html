The idea

A gift of gold, and a gift of rules

M

Mahadevaswamy gave his nephew Vignesh ₹1,00,000 and 5 rules. Vignesh ignored the rules — and lost it all on a betting tip and a random shop.

Using just the 5 rules, he built it all back. Here they are.

Rule 1 of 5

Save 1 out of every 10 rupees

Save at least 10% of what you earn — before you spend anything.

Vignesh spent first, saved whatever was left. That was always ₹0.

Rule 2 of 5

Make your gold work for you

Put savings to work — they multiply, like cattle in a field.

Mahadevaswamy never sold a calf. The herd kept growing.

He started with 3 cows.

One idli cart became three. Same idea — profits bought more, not a phone upgrade.

Rule 3 of 5

Ask people who understand money

Invest only after advice from people who are truly wise about money.

Who should Vignesh trust with his savings?

Rule 4 of 5

Don't invest in what you don't understand

Money slips away in businesses you don't understand.

Vignesh put ₹40,000 into an electronics shop. He knew nothing about electronics. It closed in 6 months.

Rule: can't explain how it makes money in one sentence? Don't invest.

Rule 5 of 5

Run from "double your money fast"

Gold runs from people chasing impossible, guaranteed returns.

A chit fund promised to double Vignesh's money in 60 days. He said no.

⚠ Warning signs

"Guaranteed" returns · "Decide today only" · No paperwork · "Keep it secret" · Paid by new investors, not real profit.

"Send ₹20,000 today, get ₹40,000 in 45 days, guaranteed."
