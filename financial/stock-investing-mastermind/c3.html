Section 3.1

Accounts Required to
Trade in the Stock Market

🏦

Account 01

Bank Account

A normal bank account where your money resides. It is the source of funds for all your stock market transactions. Money flows out of here when you buy, and flows back in when you sell.

SBI HDFC Kotak Mahindra Any bank
💻

Account 02

Trading Account

A broker account that allows you to actually place buy and sell orders in the equity and stock market. It acts as the bridge between your bank and the stock exchange. Must be opened with a registered broker.

Zerodha Motilal Oswal Upstox Groww
📂

Account 03

Demat Account

Short for Dematerialised Account. It holds the equity shares you purchase in digital form. Instead of a physical stock certificate, you receive a digital version that is automatically credited here within 2 working days of purchase.

CDSL NSDL Via your broker

Transactions

What Happens When
You Buy or Sell?

BUY Purchasing Shares

  • 1 You place a buy order through your Trading Account.
  • 2 Money is automatically deducted from your Bank Account.
  • 3 The order goes to the Stock Exchange (NSE / BSE) for execution.
  • 4 Once trade is confirmed, shares are credited to your Demat Account within 2 working days (T+2 settlement).

SELL Selling Shares

  • 1 You place a sell order through your Trading Account.
  • 2 Shares are withdrawn from your Demat Account.
  • 3 The sell order executes on the Stock Exchange.
  • 4 The sale proceeds are automatically credited back to your Bank Account.
💡

All 3 accounts are linked to each other automatically.
You only need to place an order — the system handles the money movement and share transfer on your behalf.
