Chapter 07

Board of Directors
& Management

Every company publishes a public corporate information page listing all directors, CFO, CEO, auditors, and bankers. Understanding who governs the company is your first step.

Who runs the company?

There's a clear chain of authority. Investors elect the Board of Directors, who then appoint Management (CEO, CFO) to run day-to-day operations. The board doesn't run the business — they govern it.

Chapter 06

Corporate Governance
Report

The governance report reveals how well the company is actually managed — who attends board meetings, how directors divide their attention, and what committees oversee decisions.

Director Attention: Quality Matters

A director sitting on 10 boards cannot give the same attention as one focused on just 2. Look for directors with concentrated attention — they're more likely to catch problems early and add genuine value.

Board Committees

Major decisions go to the full board. Specialized decisions go to expert committees. A director can serve on 3–4 committees simultaneously.

Chapter 08

Consolidated vs
Standalone Financials

Always look at consolidated financials. Standalone only shows the parent — consolidated shows the full picture including subsidiaries.

Standalone

When looking at financial statements, always use consolidated. This includes all subsidiaries' revenue, profit, and assets, giving you a true picture of the business.

Standalone figures can be misleading — a company might seem smaller or more profitable than it really is once you include all its arms.

📄 Auditor's Report — What to Look For

✅
Unqualified Opinion — The company's books give a true and fair view. Safe to continue research.
🚩
Qualified / Adverse Opinion — The auditor has flagged issues. As a retail investor, drop the stock immediately and move on. Don't try to rationalize.

After the auditor's report, the consolidated financials contain: Balance Sheet · Profit & Loss Statement · Cash Flow Statements · Changes in Equity · Notes to Accounts

Chapter 09

Notes to Accounts

Every line item in the balance sheet has a note number. Those notes contain the full breakdown — the story behind the numbers.

The Balance Sheet ↔ Notes Connection

Each line item shows a "Note No." — tap that note and you'll find the detailed breakdown. For example, if PPE shows ₹1,91,985 lacs — Note 3 tells you exactly how much is land vs plant vs vehicles.

Chapter 10

Notice to AGM —
Ordinary vs Special

The Annual General Meeting is your chance to participate in governance. Two types of resolutions are voted on — and Special Business demands your full attention.

ORDINARY BUSINESS

Routine Resolutions

Regular, recurring business activities. Expected and standard every year.

01 Adopt the Standalone and Consolidated Financial Statements for FY ended 31st March 2021
02 Re-appoint Mr. Dinesh Kumar Agrawal (retires by rotation and is eligible for re-appointment)
03 Re-appoint Mr. Siddharth Agrawal (retires by rotation)
04 Declaration of Final Dividend of ₹13.50/- per equity share for FY 2020-21
🟢 These are normal. Skim them and move on.
SPECIAL BUSINESS

Pay Attention Here

Non-routine activities. These can significantly affect your investment.

05
Re-appointment of Shri Abhishek Agrawal (DIN: 02434507) as Whole-Time Director for 5 years from Nov 09, 2021
⚡ Compensation change
06
Re-appointment of Shri Vinod Pillai (DIN: 00497620) as Executive Director for 5 years from June 01, 2021
⚡ Long-term commitment

🔍 Why This Matters for Investors

  • A director re-hire for 5 years locks in their pay scale
  • Check if the remuneration is linked to performance
  • Is this person contributing real value to the board?
  • Are they a promoter family member? (Related-party risk)

"If they say 'we're hoping to rehire the company's director' — it represents a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to scrutinize that hire."

Summary

7 Things Every Investor
Must Check

01
Auditor's Opinion First
Read the very last line of the auditor's report. Any qualification or adverse opinion = immediately drop the stock. No exceptions for retail investors.
02
Always Consolidated
Never analyze standalone financials alone. Consolidated = Parent + All Subsidiaries. This is the only true picture of what the business earns and owns.
03
Board Meeting Attendance
A director who skips meetings is a warning sign. Look for directors attending 6/7 or 7/7 meetings — consistent attendance shows accountability.
04
Director Directorships
Fewer boards = more focus. A director on 10+ boards gives your company about 10% attention. Prefer directors with 2–3 board seats.
05
Promoter Shareholding
High promoter holding (>50%) is usually a positive sign. But check for pledging — pledged promoter shares during financial stress can trigger forced selling.
06
Special Business at AGM
Ordinary business is routine. Special Business is where big decisions hide — director re-hires, salary changes, new capital raises. Always read these carefully.
07
Notes to Accounts
The balance sheet is just the summary. Real insights are in the notes — what makes up PPE, what are the investments, are there related-party transactions?
08
Read Multiple Reports
No investor becomes fluent from one report. Read 5–10 annual reports across different sectors. The patterns will start to emerge and you'll know what looks off.
09
Know Your Limitations
As a retail investor, you cannot verify every claim. Focus on big red flags. If something doesn't pass your smell test — there are thousands of other stocks.
