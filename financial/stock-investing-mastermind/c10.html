How to Read Shareholding — Smart Money Signal

If FII + DII ownership is consistently increasing over 8–10 quarters → smart institutional money is building confidence in the company. If it is decreasing → institutions are selling. 🔎 The "Screens" Feature — Narrowing 5000 Companies
THE PROBLEM

5,000+ Listed Companies

You can't manually research every company. Screens apply filters to instantly narrow down to companies that meet your investment criteria.

HOW IT WORKS

Custom Filter Queries

Write simple conditions like "ROE > 15 AND Debt to equity < 0.5 AND Sales growth 5yr > 20" to filter the entire market.

THE OUTPUT

A Focused Shortlist

From 5000 to perhaps 30–50 companies. These are your candidates for deeper fundamental research — saving weeks of manual work.

1
Go to Screens on Screener.in

Click "Screens" in the top navigation or visit screener.in/screens/. You'll find pre-built screens by the community as well.

2
Write Your Filter Conditions

Use plain English-style queries: "Market Cap > 500 AND ROE > 20 AND EPS growth 5yr > 15". Multiple conditions combined with AND.

3
Run & Sort Results

Click Run Query. Sort by any column — Market Cap, PE, ROCE etc. — to rank results by what matters most to you.

4
Deep-dive the Shortlist

Click each company on the shortlist to go to their full page. Now you do detailed research only on companies that already pass your filters.

A lot of features overlap. Use Screener for its superior screening/filters and quarterly breakdown. Use Ticker for social signals, IPO documents, and multi-metric peer comparison charts. Together they cover everything.

⚡ Unique Features Ticker Adds
SOCIAL SIGNAL 01

Visitor Count

How many unique visitors have viewed this company's page recently? Rising views = rising retail interest, which sometimes precedes price movement.

SOCIAL SIGNAL 02

Watchlist Count

How many Ticker users have added this to their personal watchlist? A proxy for how many investors are monitoring this stock.

SOCIAL SIGNAL 03

Smart Portfolio Count

How many users actually own this stock in their Ticker smart portfolio? Actual ownership vs just watching — shows conviction.

IPO DOCS

DRHP & RHP Documents

Draft Red Herring Prospectus and Red Herring Prospectus submitted for IPOs. Essential reading before investing in a newly listed company.

CHARTS

Multi-Chart View

Price chart, Volume chart, PE (Price to Earnings) chart, and PB (Price to Book) chart — all with 1w, 1m, 3m, 6m, 1yr, 3yr, 5yr range selectors.

PEER COMPARE

Side-by-Side Ratios

Compare similar companies on Market Cap, P/B, P/E, ROCE, EV/EBITDA simultaneously. Instantly see if the company is cheap or expensive vs peers.

Morning Star

The most in-depth of the three tools. Morningstar specialises in multi-year financial comparison, common size statements, key ratio ratings (above/below average), and Excel export for offline analysis.

↗ Open Morningstar
🌟 What Makes Morningstar Different
📋
Company Overview with Key Ratios

Quote page shows Bid/Ask, Day Range, Year Range, Forward Div Yield, Market Cap, Investment Style (Small Value / Mid Growth etc.), Beta, Price/Sales, Consensus Forward P/E, Price/Book.

📈
Performance Tab — Multi-Year Comparison

Compare profitability ratios (ROE, ROCE, Net Margin), growth rates (Revenue, EPS), and efficiency ratios year over year. See exactly how the business has evolved.

💹
Financials Tab — Full P&L, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow

Complete financial statements for multiple years. Includes operating data and valuation metrics all in one place. Extremely useful for spotting trends in margins or debt.

📐
Common Size Statements

Every line item expressed as % of Revenue (Revenue = 100%). This lets you compare margins across companies of different sizes — is COGS 60% or 75% of revenue? How does that compare to peers?

⭐
Above/Below Average Ratings via Key Ratios

Morningstar rates each company's metrics as above average, average, or below average relative to sector benchmarks for the past few years. Quick red flag detection.

📁
Excel Export

All financial data, ratios, and historical numbers can be exported to Excel. Build your own valuation models, DCF calculations, or multi-company comparison sheets.

📊
Price Vs. Fair Value

Morningstar's proprietary fair value estimate tab shows if the current market price is above or below their estimated intrinsic value — a useful secondary data point.

📏 Understanding Common Size Statements
📐

What is a Common Size Statement?

Revenue is set to 100. Every other P&L item is shown as a percentage of revenue. Example: if COGS = ₹600 Cr and Revenue = ₹1000 Cr → COGS shown as 60%. This lets you compare two companies with completely different sizes on the same scale — and spot margin expansion or compression over years at a glance.

P&L Line Item FY22 (Common Size) FY23 (Common Size) Trend Interpretation
Revenue 100% 100% Always the base
Raw Material Cost 58% 62% Rising → Margin pressure
Employee Cost 4% 3.5% Falling → Operating leverage
EBITDA Margin 22% 18% Falling → Investigate why
PAT Margin 14% 11% Falling → Red flag or cyclical?
🔗 Steps to Use Morningstar
1
Go to morningstar.in → Search Your Company

Use the search bar at top. Enter company name. Click on the correct result (verify via BSE/NSE code).

2
Start with Key Ratios Tab

Get the above-average / below-average ratings first. If a company scores below average on most metrics, it may not be worth deeper analysis.

3
Open Financials → Switch to Common Size View

Look for a "% of Revenue" toggle. Analyse margins over 5+ years. Is the company becoming more or less efficient?

4
Check Performance Tab for Growth Rates

Revenue CAGR, EPS CAGR, Dividend growth. Compare against industry averages to gauge if this is a sector leader or laggard.

5
Export to Excel for Deep Modelling

Click the Export button. Use the data to build a DCF model or custom multi-year comparison vs 3–4 peers.
