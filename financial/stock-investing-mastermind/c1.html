Why Does the Stock Market Exist?

The Problem: Idle Money

You earn a salary or profit from your business. After spending on daily needs, you have savings left over. If you just keep that money at home, it loses value every year due to inflation. And companies that need funds to grow… can't get them.

💡 The solution? The stock market acts as a bridge — connecting people who have savings with companies that need capital to grow. Everyone benefits: companies grow, jobs are created, and your savings earn returns.

The stock market is not gambling. It is a highly organised, regulated system to transfer capital from savers to businesses — and return profits back to investors.

What is a Share?

🏞️ Think of a Company as a Piece of Land

Imagine a huge plot of land worth ₹10 crore. No single person can buy it all. So you divide it into 100 equal pieces. Each piece now costs ₹10 lakh, and can be sold to different people. This is exactly how shares work.

👇 This grid = one company divided into 100 shares. Hover to explore ownership.

🔑 Key Insight: All shares of the same company are equal. Person A's 4 shares behave identically to Person B's 4 shares. Every shareholder gains or loses together — the entire company must grow for your share value to grow.
