Why Does the Stock Market Exist?

The Problem: Idle Money

You earn a salary or profit from your business. After spending on daily needs, you have savings left over. If you just keep that money at home, it loses value every year due to inflation. And companies that need funds to grow… can't get them.

💡 The solution? The stock market acts as a bridge — connecting people who have savings with companies that need capital to grow. Everyone benefits: companies grow, jobs are created, and your savings earn returns.

The stock market is not gambling. It is a highly organised, regulated system to transfer capital from savers to businesses — and return profits back to investors.

What is a Share?

🏞️ Think of a Company as a Piece of Land

Imagine a huge plot of land worth ₹10 crore. No single person can buy it all. So you divide it into 100 equal pieces. Each piece now costs ₹10 lakh, and can be sold to different people. This is exactly how shares work.

👇 This grid = one company divided into 100 shares. Hover to explore ownership.

Promoters (16 shares)
Large Investors (12 shares)
Person A (4 shares)
Person B (2 shares)
Others (66 shares)

🔑 Key Insight: All shares of the same company are equal. Person A's 4 shares behave identically to Person B's 4 shares. Every shareholder gains or loses together — the entire company must grow for your share value to grow.

What is Market Capitalisation?
Market Cap = Price per Share × Total Shares Issued

10 Cr

Your ₹2,000 share becomes ₹4,000 only when the entire company's market cap doubles — from ₹20,000 Cr to ₹40,000 Cr. Individual share price is just a slice of the total picture.

📌
Stock Split: Why Market Cap Matters More

Market Cap stays the same after a split

Reliance splits every share into 2. The price halves, but your total value doesn't change — and neither does the company's real size.

👆 Always compare companies by Market Cap, not share price. A ₹5 share of Company A is not "cheaper" than a ₹500 share of Company B — the market caps tell the real story.

🏋️ The Reliance Problem (Large Cap)

Reliance earns ₹50,000 crore profit. To double it, they need to find another ₹50,000 crore of earnings. That's incredibly hard. Meanwhile a small company with ₹50 Cr profit only needs ₹50 Cr more to double — much easier! This is why small caps can grow faster.

05
Sensex & Nifty

📏 Why do we need an Index?

You can't judge the whole market by one stock. If Axis Bank falls 3%, does that mean the entire market fell? No! An Index combines dozens of stocks with assigned "weights" and gives you one number representing the overall market health.

SENSEX

Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE)
  • Top 30 listed companies in India
  • Weights re-evaluated every 6 months
  • Largest sector: Financial Services (40.4%)
  • Reliance alone = 14.6% weight
  • HDFC Bank = 8.9% weight

NIFTY 50

National Stock Exchange (NSE)
  • Top 50 blue-chip companies
  • Combination: "National" + "Fifty"
  • Covers 4,000+ listed companies
  • Sub-indices: Nifty Bank, Nifty IT, etc.
  • Most liquid & largest companies

📊 Sensex Industry Weights (July 2022)

Sensex Change = Σ (Stock Weight × % Change) for all 30 stocks

When Sensex rises, it means most large BSE companies are doing well. When it falls, most are declining. One stock cannot move the index — it's the collective trend that matters.

06
SEBI — The Market's Watchdog

SEBI

Securities and Exchange Board of India

SEBI monitors and regulates stock exchanges, identifies loopholes, and ensures investors are never taken advantage of. Think of it as the referee of the game.

🛡️ Investor ProtectionLooks after the interests of retail investors at all times.
🚫 Anti-FraudProhibits insider trading and all unfair market practices.
📢 TransparencyEnsures companies disclose information so investors can make informed decisions.
📚 EducationPromotes financial literacy and investor education across India.
🏛️ Market DevelopmentPromotes development of the securities market.
🤝 PlatformProvides framework for brokers, advisors, and other market participants.
