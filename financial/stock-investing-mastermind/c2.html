2.1 — Understanding Earnings

What Are Earnings?

When you buy a share, you're buying a small ownership of a company's earnings. As the company grows and earns more, the share price rises too.

Size of Earnings

How Much They Earn

The total profit a company makes in a quarter or fiscal year. Infosys earning ₹200 cr has a larger size of earnings than Wipro at ₹100 cr.

Earnings Size = Revenue − Costs
Quality of Earnings

How Good the Earnings Are

Two companies earning the same ₹50 cr can have very different quality. A govt-backed EV company growing 20% y/y has far higher quality than a flat, high-competition firm.

Size of Earnings

Two Ways to Grow Earnings

Volume Increase

Sell More Units

Cadbury selling 100 units → 150 units at the same price of ₹10 each:

Before: 100 × ₹10 = ₹1,000
After: 150 × ₹10 = ₹1,500 ↑
Price Increase

Charge More Per Unit

Maggie raising price from ₹10 to ₹11 per pack (needs Pricing Power):

Before: 1000 × ₹10 = ₹10,000
After: 1000 × ₹11 = ₹11,000 ↑
Pricing Power

The Secret Weapon

Pricing power is the ability to raise prices without losing customers. Companies with unique or loved products have it. Commodity businesses don't.

High Pricing Power

Rare / Unique Product

Characteristics that create pricing power:

  • ✅ Few rivals in the market
  • ✅ No cheaper alternatives
  • ✅ Scarcity of resources
  • ✅ Strong brand (Maggi, Parle-G)
Parle-G: Price rose from ₹2 → ₹5 (2.5x) faster than inflation. People still buy it.
Low Pricing Power

Widely Available Product

Signs of weak pricing power:

  • ❌ Many competitors
  • ❌ Cheap alternatives exist
  • ❌ No brand strength
  • ❌ Abundant supply
Aviation: SpiceJet raises fares 10% → customers switch to IndiGo immediately.

2.2 — True Value & Market Value

What Is a Stock Really Worth?

Every stock has two values. One you can see on a screen. One you have to figure out yourself.

True Value (Intrinsic)

What It's Actually Worth

Based on company fundamentals — strengths, growth potential, earnings quality. This number is not quoted anywhere and is subjective. Different analysts will give different estimates for the same stock.

Market Value

What It's Trading At

The current price shown on BSE/NSE and apps like Zerodha or Groww. This fluctuates constantly based on supply, demand, and most importantly — sentiment.

Market Value = True Value + Sentimental Value

2.3 — Market Sentiments

Bull vs Bear Markets

Both bull and bear markets affect sentiment and prices — but not intrinsic value. This is the key insight.

🐂 Bull Market

Rising Prices

✅ High investor confidence
✅ Positive economic sentiment
✅ Fear of missing out (FOMO) drives buying
✅ Prices keep rising above true value
⚠️ TIP: Avoid extremely high valuations while buying
🐻 Bear Market

Falling Prices

❌ Low investor confidence
❌ Fear dominates decisions
❌ People sell to avoid further loss
❌ Prices fall below true value
⭐ TIP: This is where buying opportunities are created!
💡

The Classic Retail Investor Mistake

We should buy during bear markets (negative sentiment, low prices) and sell during bull markets (high sentiment, inflated prices). But most retail investors do the exact opposite — they buy when prices are already high due to media hype, and panic-sell when prices drop.

"When others are apprehensive and hesitant due to the bearish market, instead of being afraid we must carefully invest in stocks. When others are greedy we must be concerned and fearful." — Warren Buffett

Value Creation

When Size & Quality Go Up

A consistently performing company grows earnings year over year. Its true value increases, and eventually the market price follows. Time + Patience = Wealth.

Value Destruction

When Size & Quality Go Down

Unforeseen events (new competitors, COVID, litigation) can destroy earnings quality. Real value falls, and market price follows downward. This leads to investment losses.

Risks That Can Destroy Value
Wrong AssumptionsHigh Risk
Nestle expected to grow 15%/yr; grew only 5%. True value was lower than estimated.
Misunderstood FactsHigh Risk
Invested in petrol car company, underestimating EV adoption (now 20% market share).
Over-optimismMedium Risk
Friend said "Cello is great!" — bought without research at ₹1500 instead of fair ₹1000.
New CompetitionMedium Risk
Tata Docomo / Aircel earnings destroyed when Jio entered. Quality collapsed overnight.
Business Events (COVID, Litigation)Unpredictable
Restaurants, cinemas took massive hits. Debt piled up, real value destroyed for years.
