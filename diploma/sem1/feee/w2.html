Week 2 · Definitions & meters

Current, voltage, resistance

Five quantities, five symbols, five meters. The core relationship (Ohm's Law) has its own interactive lab in the next tab.

⚡ Go deeper: I, V, R, PD & EMF — working animations A dedicated page with a live demo + one keyword for each quantity →
I

Electric Current

Flow of free electrons through a conductor. I = Q / t

Unit: Ampere (A) · Meter: Ammeter · Live current: Tong/Clamp tester
V

Voltage / Potential

Electric pressure at a point relative to earth or the negative terminal.

Unit: Volt (V) · Meter: Voltmeter
PD

Potential Difference

Difference in pressure between two points; current flows high → low potential.

Unit: Volt (V) · Meter: Voltmeter
EMF

Electromotive Force

The force that drives current around a circuit — the source's push.

Unit: Volt (V) · Sources: Battery, DC/AC generator
R

Resistance

Opposition offered by a material to the flow of electrons.

Unit: Ohm (Ω) · Low/Med: Ohmmeter, Multimeter · High: Megger
50Hz

AC in India

Alternating current changes magnitude & direction periodically — India runs mains AC at 50 Hz.

DC examples: Battery, Solar cell · AC examples: Mains supply, AC generator

Week 2 · Circuit conditions

Open, closed, or short

Flip the switch to open/close the loop. Then try forcing a short circuit and watch what an unprotected fault path does.

Interactive circuit

Switch is OPEN — no current flows.

Open Circuit

A break or gap (or switch OFF) stops current from flowing through the entire path.

Closed Circuit

A complete, unbroken path — switch ON — lets current flow normally from source to load and back.

Short Circuit

Positive and negative terminals connect directly through near-zero resistance. Very high current flows and can damage the load.
